A. COMMITMENT

1. Regularly comes to class well - prepared, and properly communicates to students in advance changes in the scheduled meetings, if any, and reason/s for such changes.

2. Demonstrates sensitivity to students' ability to attend to and absorb content information, making the necessary in - class adjustments whenever issues in connectivity occur on the part of the teacher or the student/s.

3. Demonstrates sensitivity and flexibility to students' ability to respond to/ submit activities, assignments, and other academic requirements, adjusting the as the need arises.

4. Makes himself/herself available to students beyond official time to accomodate their questions or clarifications for asynchronous consultation.

5. Manifests knowledge of the platform/s used in flexible teaching and learning (e.g. Google Classroom, modules), and shows a positive attitude to learn more in the use of such platform.

B. KNOWLEDGE OF SUBJECT

1. Demonstrates mastery of the subject matter and does not rely solely on the prescribed reference material or online sources.

2. Demonstrates up to knowledge and/or awareness on current trends and issues on the subject.

3. Clearly explains the subject matter with depth, providing examples whenever necessary, summarizes major points, and provides proper closure so the lesson does not end abruptly.

4. Explains the relevance of present topics to the previous lessons, and relates the subject matter to relevant current issues and/or daily life activities.

5. Ensures that students gain new knowledge and/or new perspectives and acquire new skills necessary to achieve student outcomes.

C. TEACHING FOR INDEPENDENT LEARNING

1. Uses teaching strategies that allows students to think critically and to practice using the concepts taught.

2. Enhances students self - esteem and/or gives due recognition to students' performance/potentials.

3. Shows readiness in presenting the lesson using helpful audio - visual material, module, or any alternative modality to present content information that students can study or work independently.

4. Allows students to think independently and make their own decisions, and hold them responsible for their performance.

5. Encourages students to learn beyond what is required and helps/guides them on how to apply the concepts learned.

D. MANAGEMENT OF LEARNING AND RELATION WITH STUDENTS

1. Creates opportunities for intensive and/or extensive contribution of students in the class activities (e.g. allows students to class discussion)

2. Utilizes teaching approaches and innovative assessment tools, digital and non - digital tools that are responsive to varied learning styles, and gives satisfactory feedback or responses to questions to avoid confusion.

3. Deploys available flexble learning and other alternative modes of delivery. Faculty is reasonable, transparent, and outcomes - based validated.

4. Shows openness to accept conflicting beliefs yet manages to implement learning conditions that promote healthy exchange of ideas.

5. Discusses the intended learning outcomes and objectives of the course and individual topics, and structures/re-structues the teaching - learning context to enhance attainment of such outcomes.

6. Shows professionalism and an attitude worthy of respect in dealing with students in synchronous and asynchronous consultations.
